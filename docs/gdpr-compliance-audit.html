Critical Issues — Must Fix Before Next Release
🔴 Critical — GDPR Article Violations
C-1
No Cookie Consent Banner
Critical
The web app sets Supabase auth session cookies on every page load, before any user consent. The ePrivacy Directive (Cookie Law) requires informed consent before non-essential cookies are set. Although Supabase auth cookies may be classified as "strictly necessary," this has to be clearly disclosed — not assumed. No cookie consent UI exists anywhere in the codebase.

GDPR Articles violated: Article 6 (lawfulness of processing), Article 7 (conditions for consent), ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC.
Fix: Add a minimal cookie banner on first visit. Since Imotara only uses strictly-necessary session cookies (no ad-tech), the banner just needs to inform and acknowledge — no opt-out required for strictly necessary. However, a "Cookie Policy" page must exist and be linked from the banner.
Files affected: New component needed — src/components/CookieBanner.tsx + new route src/app/cookies/page.tsx
C-2
No Explicit Terms / Privacy Acceptance at Signup
Critical
Users sign up via Google/Apple OAuth without ever explicitly agreeing to the Terms of Service or Privacy Policy. There is no "I agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy" checkbox anywhere in the auth flow on either web or mobile. GDPR Article 7 requires consent to be demonstrable — implicit consent (simply using the app) does not satisfy the standard.

GDPR Articles violated: Article 7 (demonstrable consent), Article 13 (information to be provided where personal data are collected from data subject).
Fix: Add a post-OAuth "Welcome / Accept Terms" screen shown once to new users. This screen must contain clickable links to /terms and /privacy, a checkbox "I have read and agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy," and a "Get Started" button that is disabled until checked. Record acceptance timestamp in user_metadata or a consent_log table.
Files affected: imotara-mobile/src/auth/AuthContext.tsx (post-login flow), src/app/auth/callback/page.tsx (web post-OAuth redirect), new table consent_log
C-3
No Age Verification — Children's Data (GDPR Art. 8 / COPPA)
Critical
There is no age gate at signup. The Privacy Policy states "not directed to children under 13" but there is no code enforcement. GDPR Article 8 requires a lawful basis for processing personal data of children under 16 (or national minimum, typically 13–16 depending on member state) — this requires verifiable parental consent. For US users COPPA requires 13+ without parental consent.

Imotara deals with sensitive mental wellness data (mood, emotions, relationships), which falls under GDPR Article 9 "special categories of personal data" — subject to stricter processing rules regardless of age.

GDPR Articles violated: Article 8 (conditions applicable to child's consent), Article 9 (processing of special categories of personal data).
Fix: Add a date-of-birth field or "Are you 13 or older? Yes / No" at signup. If the user selects "No" — show a message that Imotara is not available for users under 13 and do not create account. For users aged 13–16 in EU countries, require a parent/guardian email for consent verification. Record DOB confirmation in user_metadata.
Files affected: imotara-mobile/src/auth/AuthContext.tsx, src/app/auth/callback/page.tsx, new src/app/onboarding/page.tsx
C-4
No Data Retention Periods Defined
Critical
GDPR Article 5(1)(e) requires personal data to be "kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary." The current privacy policy (line 243–248) vaguely states data "remains until you request deletion... or retention limits require removal" but defines no actual limits. There are no automated deletion jobs for inactive accounts or expired data.

GDPR Article violated: Article 5(1)(e) — storage limitation principle.
Fix (two parts):
1. Update Privacy Policy to specify: "Chat history and mood data: retained until you delete it or your account. Deleted accounts: all personal data purged within 30 days. Payment records: retained 7 years for tax compliance. Push notification tokens: deleted after 12 months of inactivity."
2. Create a scheduled cron job (Supabase pg_cron or Vercel cron) that runs monthly to: delete push notification tokens older than 12 months for inactive accounts; optionally flag accounts inactive > 2 years for review.
Files affected: src/app/privacy/page.tsx, new src/app/api/cron/data-cleanup/route.ts, Supabase pg_cron configuration

High-Priority Issues
🟠 High — Regulatory Risk & User Rights
H-1
No Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Documentation
High
Imotara transfers personal data to Supabase (EU/US servers), OpenAI (US), and Stripe (US). GDPR Articles 28 and 46 require a Data Processing Agreement with each sub-processor, and for US-bound transfers, either EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or an adequacy decision must be in place. None of these documents are referenced anywhere in the codebase or public-facing pages.
Fix: Add a "Sub-processors" section to the Privacy Policy listing Supabase (with their DPA link), OpenAI (their enterprise DPA), Razorpay, and Stripe. Add links to SCCs where applicable. This is documentation-only — no code changes needed.
Files affected: src/app/privacy/page.tsx
H-2
No Grievance Officer / DPO Contact (DPDPA India Requirement)
High
India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDPA) requires Data Fiduciaries to designate a Grievance Officer and publish their name and contact. The current Privacy Policy has a generic info@imotara.com contact but no named Grievance Officer. This is also best practice under GDPR for a Data Protection Officer (DPO) if Imotara processes special-category data (mood/mental wellness) at scale.
Fix: Add a "Data Protection Contact" section to the Privacy Policy with: Grievance Officer name, email (e.g., privacy@imotara.com), response time commitment (within 30 days), and escalation path (supervisory authority details for EU: each member state's DPA; for India: Data Protection Board of India).
Files affected: src/app/privacy/page.tsx
H-3
Mobile App Has No Data Export Feature
High
GDPR Article 20 grants the "right to data portability" — users must be able to receive their personal data in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format. The web app implements this via /api/export and the PrivacyActionsPanel. The mobile app has no equivalent. Mobile users cannot exercise their right to portability without accessing the web app.
Fix: Add "Export my data" to the mobile Settings screen. It should call the existing GET /api/export endpoint (already authenticated via Bearer token), receive the JSON, and offer to share it via the native Share Sheet or save to Files.
Files affected: imotara-mobile/src/screens/SettingsScreen.tsx or similar; endpoint already exists at src/app/api/export/route.ts
H-4
Account Deletion Has No Audit Trail & No Confirmation Email
High
The /api/account/delete route performs a hard-delete correctly, but: (1) There is no audit log of deletion requests — if a user disputes deletion or if there is a legal hold, Imotara cannot prove the action; (2) No confirmation email is sent to the user confirming their data has been deleted, which is a GDPR best practice and builds user trust.
Fix: Before deleting the auth user, write a tombstone record to a deletion_audit table (user_id hash, requested_at, completed_at, deletion_scope). Send a confirmation email via SMTP: "Your Imotara account and all personal data have been permanently deleted as of [date]."
Files affected: src/app/api/account/delete/route.ts, new Supabase table deletion_audit
H-5
OpenAI Receives Message Text Without Explicit Per-Session Consent
High
When the analysis consent is set to "auto" or "allow-remote," user message text is sent to OpenAI's API. The privacy policy discloses this, but: (1) The default consent mode is "auto" — meaning cloud processing begins before any explicit user decision; (2) There is no visible in-app indication during a session when a message is being processed by OpenAI vs. locally. Under GDPR, processing special-category data (mental wellness content) via a third party requires explicit consent — not just a settings disclosure.
Fix: Change default analysis consent from "auto" to "local-only". On first session, show a one-time modal: "For richer AI responses, Imotara can process your messages with cloud AI. Your text is never stored or used for training. Enable cloud AI?" — with explicit Yes/No. Record the user's choice with timestamp. Add a subtle indicator (e.g., a small cloud icon) when a message uses remote processing.
Files affected: src/lib/imotara/analysisConsent.ts line 49 (default value), src/components/imotara/AnalysisConsentToggle.tsx

Medium-Priority Issues
🟡 Medium — Best Practice & Completeness
M-1
AsyncStorage Not Encrypted (Mobile Notification Preferences)
Medium
Notification preferences in imotara-mobile/src/notifications/checkInReminder.ts lines 9–17 use unencrypted AsyncStorage. While notification preferences are low-sensitivity, this is inconsistent with the rest of the app's use of expo-secure-store and could store inferred behavioral data.
Fix: Migrate to expo-secure-store. Pattern already established in companionMemory.ts and the Supabase client.
File: imotara-mobile/src/notifications/checkInReminder.ts lines 9–17
M-2
Companion Memory Extraction Not Clearly Disclosed to Users
Medium
The companion memory system (imotara-mobile/src/state/companionMemory.ts) automatically extracts and persists personal facts from user messages (name, location, occupation, relationships). Users may not be aware this profiling occurs. The Privacy Policy does not explicitly mention "companion memory" or "fact extraction."
Fix: Add a "Companion Memory" section to the Privacy Policy. Add a Settings toggle: "Allow companion to remember facts about me" — with a "View & clear remembered facts" option. The toggle should write to the analysis consent store and be respected by companionMemory.ts.
Files: src/app/privacy/page.tsx, imotara-mobile/src/state/companionMemory.ts
M-3
Stripe Checkout Receives User Email Without Explicit Disclosure
Medium
In src/app/api/payments/stripe/checkout/route.ts line ~97, customer_email is passed to Stripe's Checkout session. The Privacy Policy does not explicitly state that the payment processor receives the user's email address.
Fix: Update Privacy Policy data sharing table to explicitly state: "Payment processors (Razorpay, Stripe) receive your email address for transaction confirmation and fraud prevention."
File: src/app/privacy/page.tsx
M-4
Azure TTS Not Mentioned in Privacy Policy
Medium
The Privacy Policy mentions OpenAI Whisper for STT transcription but does not mention Azure TTS as a fallback for text-to-speech synthesis on mobile. Text sent to Azure TTS is AI-generated companion text (not user-authored), but it should still be disclosed.
Fix: Add to the Privacy Policy sub-processors list: "Azure Cognitive Services (Microsoft): Text-to-speech synthesis for certain languages. Only AI-generated response text is processed — no user message content."
File: src/app/privacy/page.tsx
M-5
No Marketing Email Opt-In / Unsubscribe Mechanism
Medium
If Imotara ever sends promotional emails (newsletters, feature announcements), there is no opt-in mechanism at signup and no unsubscribe link in email templates. Under GDPR, promotional email requires explicit opt-in and a working one-click unsubscribe per CAN-SPAM / GDPR Article 7(3).
Fix: Add optional "Send me product updates and wellness tips" checkbox at onboarding (unchecked by default). Store preference in user_memory. Add LIST-UNSUBSCRIBE header and unsubscribe link to all non-transactional emails.
Files: New onboarding screen, email templates in src/lib/email/
M-6
No Right-to-Erasure Request Mechanism for Non-Account Users
Medium
Users who signed up but never verified email, or anonymous users with local-only data, may not be able to exercise deletion rights through the in-app flow. The career application form (api/careers/apply/route.ts) collects name and email but there is no erasure mechanism for those records.
Fix: Add a "Data Deletion Request" form at /privacy#request-deletion allowing anyone (including career applicants) to submit a deletion request by email. Manually process within 30 days. Log in a deletion_requests table.
Files: src/app/privacy/page.tsx, new form component, new API route api/privacy/erasure-request/route.ts

6.2 Add Cookie Banner Component (C-1)

// src/components/CookieBanner.tsx  — new file
"use client";
import { useState, useEffect } from "react";
const COOKIE_KEY = "imotara_cookie_consent";

export function CookieBanner() {
  const [visible, setVisible] = useState(false);
  useEffect(() => {
    if (!localStorage.getItem(COOKIE_KEY)) setVisible(true);
  }, []);
  if (!visible) return null;
  function accept() {
    localStorage.setItem(COOKIE_KEY, "accepted:" + new Date().toISOString());
    setVisible(false);
  }
  return (
    <div className="fixed bottom-4 left-4 right-4 z-50 rounded-2xl border border-white/10 bg-zinc-900/95 p-4 backdrop-blur-xl shadow-2xl max-w-lg mx-auto">
      <p className="text-sm text-zinc-300">
        Imotara uses strictly necessary cookies for authentication.
        No advertising or tracking cookies.{" "}
        <a href="/cookies" className="text-indigo-400 underline">Cookie Policy</a>
      </p>
      <button onClick={accept} className="mt-3 rounded-full bg-indigo-600 px-5 py-2 text-xs font-semibold text-white">
        Got it
      </button>
    </div>
  );
}

Mount this in src/app/layout.tsx. Create a corresponding src/app/cookies/page.tsx explaining Supabase auth cookies, localStorage usage, and absence of third-party tracking.

-- New Supabase table
CREATE TABLE consent_log (
  id           uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id      uuid REFERENCES auth.users ON DELETE CASCADE,
  consent_type text NOT NULL,   -- 'terms_v1' | 'privacy_v1' | 'cloud_ai' | 'marketing'
  granted      boolean NOT NULL,
  ip_hash      text,            -- SHA-256 of IP, not raw IP (privacy)

  created_at   timestamptz DEFAULT now()
);
ALTER TABLE consent_log ENABLE ROW LEVEL SECURITY;
-- Only service_role can write; user can read own rows
CREATE POLICY "users read own consent" ON consent_log
  FOR SELECT USING (auth.uid() = user_id);

6.6 Mobile Data Export (H-3)

// imotara-mobile/src/screens/SettingsScreen.tsx  — add export button
import * as Sharing from "expo-sharing";
import * as FileSystem from "expo-file-system";

async function exportMyData() {
  const { data, error } = await supabase.auth.getSession();
  if (!data.session) return;
  const res = await fetch(`${SUPABASE_URL}/api/export`, {
    headers: { Authorization: `Bearer ${data.session.access_token}` },
  });
  const json = await res.text();
  const path = FileSystem.cacheDirectory + "imotara_export.json";
  await FileSystem.writeAsStringAsync(path, json);
  await Sharing.shareAsync(path, {
    mimeType: "application/json",
    dialogTitle: "Save your Imotara data",
  });
}

Imotara Connect — Additional GDPR Requirements

The proposed Imotara Connect human consultancy feature introduces significantly more personal data complexity. The following GDPR obligations apply specifically to this new feature and must be built in from day one — not retrofitted:

#RequirementWhy AppliesImplementation
CN-1 Government ID Document Handling Aadhaar / Passport = sensitive personal data under GDPR Art. 9 (could infer nationality, ethnicity) Private Supabase Storage bucket + short-lived signed URLs + access limited to verified reviewer role + 7-year retention then deletion + right to access on request
CN-2 Video Call Data Video is biometric data under GDPR Art. 9 No session recording by default. Dual explicit opt-in required if recording ever enabled. Recordings (if any) must have defined retention period.
CN-3 Consultant Private Notes Notes may contain health/mental wellness data about the user Encrypted at rest, RLS (consultant-only + admin), not accessible to user but subject to deletion on erasure request
CN-4 Payment Escrow Data Links financial transactions to identifiable users Retain 7 years per tax law; must be declared in updated privacy policy sub-processors section
CN-5 DPIA Required Processing special-category data (mental health) via video with identification creates high risk Complete a Data Protection Impact Assessment before launching Connect
CN-6 Consent for Each Consultant Consultant profile (photo, bio, availability) is personal data of the consultant Consultant registration must include: explicit consent to publish their profile, right to withdraw profile (right to erasure), right to correct data
CN-7 Child Safety in Connect Human video sessions with minors require extra safeguards Hard block users under 18 from booking Connect sessions (not just 13+ general age gate)

DPIA Mandatory: Before launching Imotara Connect, a Data Protection Impact Assessment (GDPR Article 35) is legally required. The combination of: (1) special-category mental health data, (2) biometric data from video, (3) identity document storage, and (4) financial transactions — all linked to identifiable individuals — constitutes "high risk" processing under GDPR Article 35(3).
