NGO Administrator Guide — Managing Your Organisation
Confidential · July 2026
For Org Owners & Admins
Everything you need to run your Imotara organisation, in one place.
This guide walks through every module of your Imotara org admin dashboard — inviting and managing beneficiaries or staff, tracking seats, running reports for your donors, and configuring the advanced tools available to NGO accounts. Keep it handy as a reference for your whole admin team.
Your role — and who can do what
Owner
The person who created the organisation (or was assigned ownership). Full control of every module, including danger-zone actions like requesting org deletion. Usually the person Imotara's team contacts directly.
Admin
Day-to-day operator. Can invite/remove members, run reports, configure branding and integrations — everything except ownership transfer and deletion requests.
Member
A beneficiary or staff member covered by your licence. Uses the Imotara app itself — has no access to the admin dashboard at all.
How you get admin access
✓ If you created the org (via imotara.com/org/new or a seat purchase at /pricing/corporate), you're the Owner automatically.
✓ Otherwise, an existing Owner or Admin invites you by email with the "Admin" role — accept the invite and sign in at imotara.com/org/dashboard with that same email.

Before you start inviting anyone
1
Sign in at imotara.com/org/dashboard and confirm your role (Owner or Admin) and your organisation's name on the Overview tab.
2
Check your seat count and tier on Overview — this is how many beneficiaries or staff you can invite before needing more seats.
3
Have your beneficiary or staff email list ready (a spreadsheet works fine) — you'll turn it into a CSV for bulk invites on the next page.

Managing Members, License Pools & Cohorts
Confidential · July 2026
Bring your beneficiaries and staff onto their licences.
The Members tab is where you'll spend most of your time — inviting people one at a time or in bulk, managing roles, and keeping an eye on your seat count.
Inviting a single member
1
Open Members → Invite
From your dashboard sidebar, go to Members and click "Invite member."
2
Enter their email and role
Type the person's email address and choose Admin or Member.
3
They accept by email
They receive an invite email with a link. Accepting signs them in (or creates their account) and joins them to your org.
4
Their licence is assigned automatically
The moment they accept, a seat is used and they're granted your org's tier — no separate step needed.
Bulk-inviting a whole cohort by CSV
1
Prepare your file
A CSV with an "email" column (a "role" column is optional — defaults to Member).
2
Upload in Members → bulk import
Drag and drop the file, or use the file picker.
3
Review the preview
Each row is checked and flagged as valid or invalid before you send anything.
4
Send and review results
Up to 500 invites per batch. Any failures (already a member, malformed email) are reported per row so you can fix and resend just those.
Roles, removal & seats
✓ Change a member's role any time (Admin ⇄ Member) from their row in the Members list.
✓ Removing a member frees their seat immediately and revokes their org-level licence.
✓ A member's own personal Imotara subscription (if they have one) is never downgraded by removal or by your org's tier.
✓ Your seat counter ("X / Y used") is always visible — invites are blocked once you're at capacity until a seat frees up or more are purchased.
🌐
Let people join without waiting for an invite
In Settings → Domain Auto-Join, list your organisation's email domain(s) (e.g. yourgo.org) and switch it on. Anyone signing up with a matching email can join your org directly — useful for large or fast-moving cohorts where sending individual invites isn't practical.
Not every member needs the same tier — license pools
✓ Imotara's team can issue your organisation one or more named pools (e.g. "200 Pro seats — Case Workers") with a set quantity and optional expiry, so your programme isn't stuck on one blanket tier.
✓ You, as org admin, assign individual pool licences to specific members from the Pools module — and withdraw them just as easily if someone leaves that programme.
✓ A member can only hold one active pool assignment at a time; withdrawing frees that unit back into the pool for reuse.
✓ If tiers ever overlap, Imotara always resolves it the same way, automatically: pool assignment first, then a per-member override, then a personal subscription, then your org's default tier, then Free.
Cohorts — organising members into sub-teams
✓ Create a cohort for any sub-group you manage separately — a field office, a programme, an age band (e.g. "Delhi Field Team", "Under-18 Outreach").
✓ Add members to a cohort — they must already be an active member of your organisation first.
✓ Set a companion tone policy per cohort, restricted to Close Friend, Calm Companion, Coach or Mentor — useful for programmes with specific care guidelines.
✓ Cohorts are an organisational view for your team, not a separate billing unit — seats still come from your org's pool or licence.

Generating an Impact Report for donors
1
Open the Certificate / Impact Report page
Available once your org has been active 30+ days with at least 10 seats in use.
2
Review your aggregate stats
Real usage data for your org — weekly active members, engagement trends — with no individual identities anywhere in it.
3
Print or save it
The report opens as a print-ready page — use your browser's Print → Save as PDF to keep a copy for your grant file or donor pack.
Audit Log
✓ Every invite, role change, seat change and removal is logged with a timestamp and who performed it.
✓ Browse the full history, paginated, from the Audit Log tab.
✓ Export the whole log to CSV any time — useful for your own compliance records.
Custom branding
✓ Settings → Branding: set a logo URL, an accent colour (any hex value), and a brand name.
✓ These appear across your org's admin dashboard — your sidebar and reports carry your organisation's identity.
ℹ️
Scope of branding today
Branding customises your org admin dashboard. It does not currently change the Imotara app your beneficiaries use — they see the standard Imotara experience regardless of your org's branding.
Embedding Imotara in your own site
1
Open the Embed module
A persistent embed key is generated automatically for your organisation.
2
Allow-list your domain(s)
Add the domain(s) where you'll embed Imotara — for example your own portal, intranet or LMS.
3
Copy the iframe snippet
Paste the ready-made snippet into your site to embed an Imotara chat window directly.
Generating an API key
✓ API Keys tab → generate a new key. Keys are hashed and shown to you only once — store it securely.
✓ Two scopes only: read:stats (aggregate analytics) and read:members (your member list) — no write access, no conversation data.
✓ Use a key to pull data into your own M&E dashboard or reporting pipeline; revoke any key instantly if it's no longer needed.

Verification, Contracts, SSO & Getting Help
Confidential · July 2026
A few things your team sets up once.
Registration verification, contract storage, and single sign-on — plus where to go when you need a hand.
Submitting registration verification
1
Host your registration document
Upload your 80G / FCRA / Section 8 (or your country's equivalent) proof to a service you already use — Google Drive, Dropbox, etc. — and copy a shareable link.
2
Paste it into the Verification module
Add the document link, choose a document type, and add any notes for context.
3
Imotara's team reviews it
Your status shows as "pending review" and Imotara's team is notified automatically to review it by hand.
⏳
This is a link-based submission, not a file upload
There's no drag-and-drop upload box in the dashboard today — host the file yourself and share the link. Review is manual, not automatic.
Storing contracts & SLAs
✓ Contracts module: add a link to any signed agreement (already hosted elsewhere), its type, signed date and valid-until date.
✓ Useful as a shared record for your team and Imotara's account contact — same link-based approach as Verification.
Single sign-on (SSO)
🔐
Configuration today, not yet self-serve login
You can save your identity provider's details (entity ID, SSO URL, certificate, email domain) in the SSO module — this puts your request into "pending" status. Actual SSO sign-in requires Imotara's team to complete setup on our side; it isn't instant or automatic yet. Contact info@imotara.com after submitting to start that process.
Frequently asked questions
Q An invite email never arrived — what now?
Ask them to check spam, or remove and re-invite from the Members tab. You can also turn on Domain Auto-Join so matching emails can join without an invite at all.
Q A member already has a personal Imotara plan?
It's never downgraded. If your org's tier is higher, they get the benefit of both while their membership with you is active.
Q We've run out of seats — what are our options?
Free up a seat by removing an inactive member, or contact your account owner about purchasing more seats or requesting a License Pool.
Q Can anyone on our team see what a beneficiary discusses?
No — not even Owners or Admins. Every view in the dashboard is aggregate only. Individual conversations are never exposed at the organisation level.
